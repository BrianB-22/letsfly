✈ About SimLetsFly

SimLetsFly started as a personal tool to break out of flying the same old routes — a habit that's easy to fall into going back to the FSX days. With the incredible scenery in today's simulators, there's a whole world worth exploring. This app makes it easy to discover flights you'd never think to plan yourself. All airports are compatible with MSFS 2020.

How It Works

Set your search parameters and hit Let's Fly!. SimLetsFly picks a random departure and arrival that match your criteria. Not happy? Hit it again or lock your departure and randomize only the arrival.

SimLetsFly is not a full flight planner — once you have your airports, use MSFS or your preferred tools to plan the actual route (VFR/IFR, SID/STAR, etc). Think of it as your destination picker.

Departure Options
Enter Specific Airport
Type an ICAO code and hit Validate, or search by airport name or city to find the ICAO if you don't know it.
Favorite Airports
Pin airports you fly from regularly. Requires a free account. The app randomly picks one of your pinned airports as departure.
Random — Match Criteria
Let SimLetsFly pick a random departure based on your location, airport type, runway, and fuel filters.
Search Parameters
Location
Continent, Country, Region/State, or fully Random
Range
Min and Max distance in nautical miles between departure and arrival
Airport Size
Small, Medium, Large, Seaplane Base, Heliport
Runway Surface
Hard (paved), Soft (grass/gravel), Water, Helicopter pad
Runway Length
Min and Max runway length in feet
Fuel
Filter by AvGas and/or Jet-A availability
Nav Aids (Arrival)
Filter arrival airports by ALS (approach lighting), VASI/PAPI, and/or ILS availability
Flight Results
Airport Cards
Each result shows ICAO, name, type, elevation, max runway, fuel, nav aids, and live METAR weather.
METAR Weather
Live METAR is fetched for both airports when a flight is generated. Click Decode to get a plain-English breakdown of the raw METAR string.
Flight Summary
Shows distance in nautical miles, bearing with a compass rose, estimated flight time based on your cruise speed, and a route map.
Cruise Speed
Set your aircraft's cruise speed (knots) to get an accurate estimated flight time. Logged-in users have their speed saved automatically.
Next Hop
Chain flights together — Next Hop sets your current arrival as the next departure and finds a new random arrival.
Saving & Sharing
Save Flight
Saves the current flight to your account. Requires a free account.
Copy to Clipboard
Copies a full flight brief to your clipboard including airport details, runway info, fuel, nav aids, METAR, distance, bearing, and estimated time. Great for pasting into a notes app before flying.
Share Flight
Saves the flight as public and copies a shareable link. Anyone with the link can open it and see the same flight — they can also save it to their own account.
My Flights (Logbook)

Access your saved flights at My Flights in the header (requires sign in). Your logbook lets you:

Rate Your Flight
1–5 stars across 8 categories: Takeoff, Landing, Procedures, Navigation, Scenery, ATC, Fun Factor, and Overall.
Pilot Notes
Add free-form notes about the flight — weather encountered, challenges, memorable moments.
Mark Completed
Track which flights you've actually flown vs. flights saved for later.
Route Map
View an interactive map of any saved flight showing departure and arrival positions.
Public Sharing
Toggle any saved flight to Public and copy a share link. Other pilots can open it, see your notes and ratings if you leave them, and save the flight to their own account.
