✈ About SimLetsFly

SimLetsFly started as a personal tool to break out of flying the same old routes — a habit that's easy to fall into going back to the FSX days. With the incredible scenery in today's simulators, there's a whole world worth exploring. This app makes it easy to discover flights you'd never think to plan yourself. Most airports should be available in commercial flight simulators.

How It Works

Set your search parameters and hit Let's Fly!. SimLetsFly picks a random departure and arrival that match your criteria. Not happy? Hit it again or lock your departure and randomize only the arrival.

SimLetsFly can also generate an IFR route between your airports using real-world airway data, and export it directly to your simulator. See the Pull Route & Export section below.

Departure Options
Enter Specific Airport
Type an ICAO code and hit Validate, or search by airport name or city to find the ICAO if you don't know it.
Favorite Airports
Pin airports you fly from regularly. Requires a free account. The app randomly picks one of your pinned airports as departure.
Random — Match Criteria
Let SimLetsFly pick a random departure based on your location, airport type, and runway filters. If you're signed in, your last-used filters are restored automatically on login.
Search Parameters
Location
Continent, Country, Region/State, or fully Random
Range
Min and Max distance in nautical miles between departure and arrival
Airport Size
Small, Medium, Large, Seaplane Base, Heliport
Runway Surface
Hard (paved), Soft (grass/gravel), Water, Helicopter pad
Runway Length
Min and Max runway length in feet
Cruise Speed & Airways
Set your cruise speed in knots for estimated flight time. Choose High or Low airways — High for jets (FL180+), Low for props and GA aircraft.
Nav Aids (Arrival)
Filter arrival airports by ALS (approach lighting), VASI/PAPI, and/or ILS availability
Flight Results
Airport Cards
Each result shows ICAO, name, type, elevation, location (region, country, continent), nav aid tags, and live METAR weather.
Runway List
Each airport card shows all individual runways with length, surface type, and nav aids (VASI/PAPI, ALS) labeled per runway end. Once METAR loads, the recommended runway is shown based on current wind direction with headwind and crosswind components.
METAR & TAF Weather
Live METAR is fetched for both airports when a flight is generated. Click Decode for a plain-English breakdown. A TAF forecast is also shown below the METAR where available — useful for planning ahead on longer flights.
Weather Radar
The route map shows a live weather radar overlay pulled from RainViewer. The most recent radar frame updates automatically when a flight is generated.
Flight Summary
Shows distance in nautical miles, bearing with a compass rose, estimated flight time based on your cruise speed, and an interactive route map.
Multi-Leg Flights
Use Next Hop to chain flights together. Each leg appears in the flight plan list. Click any leg to load it into the search results — then use Pull Route and Export on that specific leg.
Pull Route & Export

SimLetsFly can generate a real-world IFR route between your airports using airway data from Flight Plan Database, then export it directly to your simulator.

Pull Route Button
Located in the Pilot Notes header. Fetches both a High airways route (for jets, FL180+) and a Low airways route (for props/GA). Both are displayed in the notes — the one matching your current High/Low airways setting is shown first. Route is formatted as waypoints and airways separated by dashes, e.g. APE24 - J152 - BLISS.
Export ▾ Dropdown
Located in the bottom action bar. Opens a menu with the following options:
SimBrief Opens SimBrief with your departure and arrival pre-filled for full IFR planning. No route pull required.
X-Plane 12 Downloads a .fms flight plan file ready to load in X-Plane 12's FMS. Requires Pull Route first.
X-Plane 11 Downloads a .fms file in X-Plane 11 format. Requires Pull Route first.
MSFS 2020 Downloads a .pln XML file for Microsoft Flight Simulator 2020. Requires Pull Route first.
MSFS 2024 Downloads a .pln XML file for Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024. Requires Pull Route first.
Multi-Leg Export
Export works on one leg at a time. If you have multiple hops, click the leg in the flight plan list to load it into the results window, then Pull Route and Export for that leg. Repeat for each leg.
Saving & Sharing
Save Flight
Saves the current flight to your account. Requires a free account. Each time you click Let's Fly while signed in, your search preferences are also saved — location filters, airport types, runway surface, range, cruise speed, and display theme — and restored automatically next time you log in.
Copy to Clipboard
Copies a full flight brief to your clipboard including airport details, runway info, fuel, nav aids, METAR, distance, bearing, and estimated time. Great for pasting into a notes app before flying.
Share Flight
Saves the flight as public and copies a shareable link. Anyone with the link can open it and see the same flight — they can also save it to their own account.
My Flights (Logbook)

Access your saved flights at My Flights in the header (requires sign in). Your logbook lets you:

Rate Your Flight
1–5 stars across 8 categories: Takeoff, Landing, Procedures, Navigation, Scenery, ATC, Fun Factor, and Overall.
Pilot Notes
Add free-form notes about the flight — weather encountered, challenges, memorable moments. Pull Route results are also saved here when you use them.
Mark Completed
Track which flights you've actually flown vs. flights saved for later.
Route Map
View an interactive map of any saved flight showing departure and arrival positions.
Flight Stats
The top of My Flights shows running stats across all your saved flights — total distance, completed flights, regions explored, longest trip, average leg distance, and more.
Public Sharing
Toggle any saved flight to Public and copy a share link. Other pilots can open it, see your notes and ratings if you leave them, and save the flight to their own account.
Your Data & Privacy

SimLetsFly is a personal hobby project. Here's exactly what is stored if you create a free account:

  • Your email address (used for sign-in only)
  • Saved flights, ratings, and pilot notes you choose to add
  • Search preferences and cruise speed (to restore your setup on login)
  • Favorite airports you pin

An anonymous page visit counter runs on each page load — no personal data is collected, just a running total. No tracking scripts or advertising analytics are used. Account data is stored securely via Supabase. You can request deletion of your account and all associated data at any time by leaving a note in the feedback box below.
